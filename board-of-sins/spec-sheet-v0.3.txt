BOARD OF SINS
Technical Specification Document
v0.3 Draft  ·  Project Lead: sintaxsaint
Everything the Pi does. And more.
1. Project Overview
The Board of Sins is an ambitious single-board computer (SBC) designed to outperform the Raspberry Pi 5 at a comparable price point, while maintaining full GPIO HAT compatibility and transparent ethical pricing. Built around the Rockchip RK3588 SoC, it targets makers, developers, and AI enthusiasts who need more power without paying premium prices.

Core Philosophy
•	More capable than the Raspberry Pi 5 in every measurable way
•	Transparent cost-plus pricing at 1.5x markup — no hidden margins
•	Full Raspberry Pi HAT compatibility via standard 40-pin GPIO header with hardware-level compatibility — no software workarounds required
•	Ethical alternative to a foundation that abandoned its original mission
•	Designed to scale — start at £100, drive price down as volume grows
•	Black PCB across all models — clean, sleek aesthetic
•	Part of the Restore project — returning computing to a golden age of openness with modern hardware

2. System-on-Chip
Rockchip RK3588
The RK3588 is the highest-performance SBC-class SoC available for open board design. Its combination of high core count, wide memory bus, and built-in NPU makes it the only realistic choice for the Board of Sins specification.

Built-in NPU	6 TOPS (INT4/INT8/INT16/FP16) — RKNN supported

3. Memory
RAM — Sourcing Status & Strategy
LPDDR4 at the densities required for a cost-effective SBC does not flow through standard open distribution channels such as LCSC, Mouser, or Digikey. The AI data centre boom has consumed available supply, with memory manufacturers selling primarily through direct ODM relationships. This is a known structural constraint affecting all independent SBC developers, not a project-specific failure.

The Board of Sins v0.3 adopts a pragmatic two-phase memory strategy:

•	Phase 1 (Prototype): Source discrete LPDDR4 dies through direct manufacturer outreach or a Shenzhen-based contract manufacturer with existing supply relationships. Target: validated x32 dies on the Rockchip BSP DDR support list.
•	Phase 2 (Production): Establish direct supply agreements at volume. Higher RAM tiers (8GB, 16GB, 32GB) will follow once the base model is proven and supplier relationships are in place.

Specification	Detail
Interface	64-bit dual channel LPDDR4/LPDDR4X (2x 32-bit channels)

4. AI Acceleration
The Board of Sins targets on-device AI inference without cloud dependency. The primary AI capability is the RK3588’s built-in 6 TOPS NPU, which is present on every model from launch. Additional AI acceleration via M.2 is available to users who choose to add a compatible accelerator card.

Hailo Supply Status
Direct access to Hailo-8 silicon requires a verified company email address, making it inaccessible to the project at its current stage. Rather than delaying launch, the Board of Sins will ship with the M.2 slot available for user-supplied AI acceleration. When a supply agreement with Hailo or an equivalent vendor is finalised, all early adopters will be contacted and offered a free AI accelerator expansion card shipped to their door. Boards sold after the deal closes will include the card as standard.

9. Development Roadmap
Phase 1 — Parts Selection & Specification
•	SoC confirmed: RK3588. Ports confirmed. RAM sourcing strategy defined (direct manufacturer outreach required).
•	Finalise PMIC (RK806 likely), Wi-Fi module, thermal solution
•	Resolve GPIO level shifting requirements — reference Rock 5B schematic
•	Begin outreach to Hailo and equivalent AI chip vendors
•	All specification and parts decisions documented publicly under the Restore project

Phase 2 — PCB Design & Prototype
•	Design schematic in EasyEDA based on RK3588 reference design
•	Route PCB — LPDDR4, USB high-speed, HDMI differential pairs, GPIO level shifting
•	Validate against Rockchip design guidelines
•	Send for prototype fabrication — black PCB

Phase 3 — Production & Public Launch
•	Professional PCB assembly — not hand-soldered
•	Volume component purchasing to drive down BOM cost
•	Public launch at £100 retail
•	Ship AI accelerator expansion cards to early adopters once supply deal finalised
•	Scale volume to reduce price toward and below Raspberry Pi pricing
